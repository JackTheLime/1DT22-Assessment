WHAT IS CORAL BLEACHING?

So what is coral bleaching. Bleaching is the process of the algae known as zooxanthellae living within the tissues of coral being expelled from the coral and turning the it a white, ghostly colour.

These algae are responsible for photosynthesis, leading to food production for the coral so they can grow into great, lucious reefs like the ones we know today. Reasons for the bleaching happening can be attributed to many factors; Blockage of sunlight, polluted waters changing the chemical balance or the most well known and most attributed cause of bleaching, the rapid change in temperature. When the algae gets expelled, it isn't just because the water is too hot for it to survive, but because the algae is adaptive to the water temperature meaning it reproduces oxygen reactive to species of itself.

In the past 100 years the ocean has gone through a change of 0.13ºC hotter, from quick glance, it doesn't sound like much, but if you take into consideration how constant the ocean water temperatures have been for over 1,000 years, it is a massive change for the worst. In 2016-2017, these temperature changes were sudden on the oceanfront, we lost a third of our precious coral reefs in the Great Barrier Reef located in Australia, this third or coral was over 2,100km of coral, fully bleached never to regain colour in the human lifespan.
